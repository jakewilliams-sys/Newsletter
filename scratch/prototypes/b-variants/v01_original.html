The social ordering effect

The biggest surprise from the immersion research: social ordering (splitting a basket with flatmates) accounts for over a third of student transactions. This behaviour is almost absent in the general population and suggests a product opportunity around group ordering features.

Students also show a distinct price sensitivity threshold that sits lower than the general population but higher than previously assumed — they're willing to pay for convenience when the social context justifies it.
