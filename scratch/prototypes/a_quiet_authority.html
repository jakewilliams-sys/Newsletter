Students order differently — and more often than we thought

A deep-dive into student ordering behaviour reveals key drivers of adoption and frequency among 18–24 year olds, with implications for growth strategy and Plus positioning.

Jake Williams · March 2026

Student Immersion Research

Students don't just order more frequently — they order at different times, in different group sizes, and with a distinct price sensitivity threshold that sits lower than the general population but higher than previously assumed. The biggest surprise: social ordering (splitting a basket with flatmates) accounts for 34% of student transactions.
