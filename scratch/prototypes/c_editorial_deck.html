Students order differently — and more often than we thought

A deep-dive into student ordering behaviour reveals key drivers of adoption and frequency among 18–24 year olds. The biggest surprise: social ordering — splitting a basket with flatmates — accounts for 34% of student transactions, a behaviour nearly absent in the general population.
